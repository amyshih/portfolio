[ YOUR TRAVELING BUDDY ]

Role:

Thesis Researcher

Duration:

1 year

Principle investigator:

Seng-cho T. Chou, Ph.D.

Research Method:

Grounded Theory

Research Base:

5 Travel agencies (Lion Travel, Phoenix Travel, ezTravel, LifeTour) and 15 representative customers


Problem Analysis

This thesis applied Grounded Theory to conduct qualitative research in the most influential e-commerce consumer group — travel agencies. Through interviews with managers of the top-5 travel agencies and representative clients in Taiwan, I analyzed and summarized the concept of Power Shifting. The expectation gap between customers' demand and existing online services of travel agencies may enhance customer’s negative emotion and in turn magnify the gap to cause the so-called negative stimulus. The negative stimulus urges customers to look for ways to neutralize the dissatisfaction from travel agencies. Moreover, if the agencies are not able to handle the situation timely, customers have to deal with the issues by themselves. After the emergent issue settles, the customers would evaluate the agencies’ services or compensation based on their experiences and thus adjusting their dissatisfaction to the agencies.


According to literatures, there are three aspects of advices to meet both managers’ and customers’ demands: specialization, value co-creation and mass customization. In response to characteristics of live chat (Online supporting system), four principle guidelines and feasible service encounters are summarized. Based on Berry’s three aspects of relationship marketing, Taiwanese travel agencies devote most of their resources to financial bonds, namely to retain customers by practicing pricing strategies. This thesis aim to improve models of customer service by upgrading to higher aspects, social bond and structural bond.

Service Gap Solution

When I checked the customer journey, I found there is an obvious service gap on customer journey map, in which this thesis aims to improve this gap by Live chat services. So far, travel agencies only are informed with issues if customers file complaints; however, they are overlooking those unfiled messages. live chat services actually can be introduced when customers initiate motivation on travelling and guide them to choose products that best fit their requirements. In this case, live chat is able to record every detail, demands and hesitation, of the customer in the beginning for future advancement.


Live Chat Service Improvement : Mobility

During the interviews, almost every manager mentioned plans of introducing mobile devices to their services in the near future. This thesis proposes a concept of online mobility customer service, which aims to provide instant assistance for customers throughout the journey.


Before the trip

The prototype of the new mobile online live chat system helps customers locate the nearest travel agent via Location-Based Service (LBS) or even reach out to customer service via voice calls or text messages on mobile phones. This service focuses on the gap from customers have incentive of traveling to the moment they contact to the travel agency spontaneously. From passive to active, this function aims to encourage travel agencies reach customers ss not only from passive-marketing ads, but also provide useful informations to customers.


Index of travel agencies live supporting app

Customers can plan their trips through this app. The travel agency can apply mass customization to recommend related travelling plans.

The most importantly, customers can reach the customer services anytime.


During the trip

All along the journey the service could stay beyond physical and time zone limits, customer service executives can actively advise some travel information based on customers’ location. Furthermore, if there are any emergency, customers are able to reach customer services for immediate assistance.

After the trip

Most customers are lack of motivations to give feedbacks after the journey, which is a lost in finding major issues of services for travel agencies. To advance the quality of services, agencies now can retrieve photos from mobile devices to send regards to their customers and meanwhile to get direct customer feedbacks.



Earthquake Education Redesign

Based on Gamification

[ Learn Hard, Play Hard! ]

Role:

UX Designer

Duration:

6 months

Principle investigator:

NTU iNSIGHT - Yi-Sing Deng

Team member:

Mei-Ching Shih, Ko-Pu Lin, Ping Lee, Wei-Hung Chen, Yue-Ying Lee,Yu-Tzu Lin, Yu-Chen Ching, Yu-Ching Liu, Si-Miou Chen, Dan-Ning Yang



Design Concept

Located at Circum-Pacific Seismic Zone, Taiwanese encounter earthquakes frequently. The related disasters such as tsunami, fire and power outage always make people be injured. After the horrible 921 Earthquake, the 7.6 Mw earthquakes, aided more than 100,000 lost their home and got injured, Taiwanese government decided to teach the disaster prevention strategies in K-12 education. However, although children know the importance of the disaster protections, the existing teaching materials and methods could not stimulate them. Our team aimed to use design-thinking mindset to rethink the teaching methods and scenerios. We aim to develop solutions for children to learn the practical knowledge with willingness instead of antipathy.



Design Thinking Process

| Design Thinking + Gamification

The project conducted user researches by using design thinking method. After interviewed students in elementary school and their teachers. We went through four workshops for finding insights to the final design.


| Empathize

After we defined the goal of the project, we used affinity diagram to gather and organize large amounts of data by evidencing their natural correlations. The post-it became physical instruments to find correlations and identify the significant groups. And we also listed out the experience journey of children from they started the class of earthquake prevention, including what they were thinking, feeling and doing. Also we listed the stakeholders' actions during this process.



| Define

In this session, we discussed personas of students, teachers, and parents. After we listed the important features of personas, we further defined the key issues for this project. We also determined the scope of the project, we would focus on the disaster education on students, teachers, parents and also local geography.



| Ideation

In ideation stage we generated crazy ideas based on the key issues we stated on the last stage. By communicating with drawing and talking, we categorized and narrowed our common goal iteratively. After we defined final ideas, we used story chain method to build up a serial story. And the scenarios and service touch points were defined through the process.



| Concept Developed and Consolidated



Our value proposition for the finished product is :

  • Lower the using barrier
  • Enlarge the amount of participation
  • Emphasize localize information

We designed a board game, Concrete Jungle, based on local geography to engage children with learning through game. We aim to enhance children’s preparation of disaster prevention. We hope teachers and parents can join in this game. Children as a player need to group up with other children as a team and beat disasters. By playing as several disasters that children will encounter after earthquakes happened, teachers and parents can give hints and directions during children discuss their strategy to run to safe places.


Prototype Features


Local Character  |

We designed a Taiwanese endangered animal, Leopard cat, as the character in the game. We gave this adorable character because we hope children can also recognize this endangered species, and teach them to protect our environment through playing this game.


Customize map  |

The map customized 5 miles (compared to real map, each 2.5 kilometers equal to 360 centimeters in the prototype) around the target school, and also shows real geographical information. The map includes the place to prevent from danger such as hospital, empty spaces. And the scopes of the disasters also show on the map, when players locate near the injured areas, they need to generate strategies to run to the safe place. We hope students learn how to run for their life.

Tool Cards  |

Players can get various cards from every round. The cards contain special functions that encourage players develop their own strategy to win the game (avoid the disasters and first arrive the safe place.)



Disaster Chess  |

Disaster chess shows the common disasters, including fire, flood and earthquake. It also reveals the affected scope that will hurt the people near it.


Usability Testing

After we finished the prototype of Concrete Jungle, we conducted usability testing to have further feedback from real potential users. We gave the game to a family include a pair of parents, a 9-year-old boy, and a 7-year-old girl. We did not teach them the rule at first. They took over 30 minutes to understand the rule. We found many crucial points that we still need to improve from only communicating with users through the paper of rules.After we taught them the rule, the family really enjoyed the game. We observed that when the children just see the customized map, they can recognize the places near their school. Also, the children can actually connect the strategy in the game to real-world disaster situation.



Future Work

After the usability testing, I listed out several guidelines for future revise work.

  • The rule should be easier to understand for K-12 students, it can also add Pin-Yin to facilitate them to understand.
  • The disaster-affected scope can be printed on the chess directly. In this case, players do not need to check the rule cards repeatedly.
  • Parents are children’s partners in the game, they could join the game in other way. For example, parents could not only be the disasters to control the affected scope, they can also play as children's partners to discuss with their small partners and stimulate children's learning incentive more.

DON' TV

[ Body is your controller ]

Role:

Chief Programmer

Duration:

1 year

Principle investigator:

Wei-Po Lee, Ph.D.

Team member:

Mei-Ching Shih, Chun-Shan Lin, Shan-Ru Lin, I-Ling Chen, Yu-Fang Lin, Yu-Huan Liu

Development tool:

Visual Studio 2010、.NET Framework、Windows server 2008、Microsoft Kinect SDK beta

Programming tool:

Visual C#、Microsoft SQL Server



Design Concept

Over the years, as we have grown to be increasingly dependent on technology, the importance of interactive design raises. When Smart TV has been emerging, online add-on services are featured on top of traditional functions of TV programs. However, the design of Smart TV remote controller is too complicated for users to handle compared with conventional ones. This project aim to provide the intuitive way of controlling our entertainment product, TV, with integration of motion sensing technology and Smart TV to enable straightforward controls by simple hand signals. Moreover, services of social networking and personalized recommendation are consolidated to redefine physical limitations and elevate user experiences in intelligent life.


KEY FACTOR

This system furnishes with Microsoft Kinect motion sensors to detect users’ signals and control Windows system-simulated Smart TV. It replaces traditional controllers with users’ intuition hand signals, which also avoids problems in complicated operations or finding the controllers.



System Analysis



    First-Login Guide

    Onboarding Page provides assistance to new users by following instruction to imitate corresponding hand signals one after another. This helps users act correctly in operating Smart TV.




    Instant Image Recognition Service

    Instant image recognition service enables users to touch on a specific person or object and search it online using key word or image search from Google when watching TV.


    Personalized Knowledge Recommendation

    This system makes program recommendations to users based on their detention period and frequency of visit. Additionally, the collected information is used to conduct Pearson Correlation Analysis with the group of people having similar interests, thus the system also recommends channels popular among people with the same  hobbies. Another special mode provides a list of the most popular channels that users can choose from.


    SYSTEM DEMO

    (The video shows the prototype of the system, those are slightly different with the final scene. This video focus on the functions of this system.)




    Future Goal - Avatar Interaction

    Imagine in the near future, when we are watching TV with our families. We can simply do a guesture, the channel will change to the next. In addition, we can also play with our friends when we are watching TV, even we are not watching TV at the same place. The avatar interaction function will show below the television and follow users' body motions. We can also clap for the nice catch in the ball game, or we can throw pie to the bad women in the soap opera. DON' TV aims to break the barrier of spaces and make people well connected.



DIG Brainstorming Platform

[ CROSS YOUR IDEA's BOARDER ONLINE ]

Role:

System Analyst

Duration:

4 months

Director:

Industrial Technology Research Institute of Taiwan - Fang-I Chen

Team member:

Industrial Technology Research Institute of Taiwan - Mei-Ching Shih, William T. Y. Huang, Chia-Yin Chang, Kevin Yang, Pei-Lin Chen, Chi-Siang Wu


Design Concept

In the era of information explosion, the industries often find themselves facing these common difficulties.

  • Lacking a defined vision of innovation and uncertain strategies for providing better product values for the customers.
  • Different divisions are hard to reach consensus.
  • Possessing advanced technology yet having difficulty on finding suitable application areas.

Innovation is the key to create customer values and organizational competitiveness.The Dechnology Idea Generator (DIG) platform was created to resolve the innovation difficulties. The goal of DIG is to assist the organizations by providing online services and helping them utilizing design thinking combine with their core competitive technologies, and further to comprehend a better market demand trend and innovative decision planning.


Dechnology Methodology

The term “Dechnology” referred to an innovative concept proposed by the Industrial Technology Research Institute of Taiwan (ITRI). The idea is to assist organizations defining their opportunity in the market, through the combination of design thinking and technology innovation

| Human-Centered Experience Design

Driving customer insights through user studies and explore innovative opportunities, then combine the core technology and business knowledge to create new product with better user experiences, thus creating market demand.


| Technology Commercialized Design

Based on the core technologies, the corporation would explore the feasibility of cross-market application fields and thus creating new business model and fulfilling the market demand.


Target Audience

| Innovative Product Managers

People who are in search of a market-demanded product.



| Researchers and Developers

People who possess advanced technology and are looking for practical application fields.



Platform Feature

The goal of the platform aims to achieve the human-centered mindset by conducting design thinking approach. It helps users to understand consumers, meanwhile also help them review their on-hand technology assets and their potential market demands.


The platform can be generalized in three areas: Persona, Trend and Technology. The user may conduct information collection and brainstorming activity on DIG platform. The system also analyzes highly relative consumer demands and technology trends in different fields based on the characteristics of the user’s project.


The platform system would collect information to the creative board and assist project planners to derive divergent and convergent thoughts. Also, online experts would also be available; users may access the consultant services without temperal and spacial limitations, pathing the ways for more innovative ideas.


System Analysis


| Scenario study - Story from the Material and Chemical Research Laboratory in ITRI

The Material and Chemical Research Laboratory of ITRI has long proposed an advanced technology of manufacturing hollow fiber membranes. However, the technical staffs have been in the related field for many years and were facing innovation difficulties. Through the DIG platform the research staff were able to set a initial potential user and also the core technology they had. After setting, the creative board would recommand related trending and application information that enable the research team to brainstorm and provide alternative insights for the possibility of innovative products.


On the other hand, a dehumidifier company was in distress of being uncertain on their new product design plan. Based on the targeted consumer and market demand that the company’s project manager has input in the DIG platform, the system was able to analyze the key word “hollow fiber” and depict the technology’s applications and trends through user-friendly descriptions. Due to the online expert are available, the dehumidifier company talked to the expert and found they can collaborate with the Material and Chemical Research Laboratory of ITRI by using the hollow fiber technique to update the quality of their dehumidifier. As the result, the company found a surprising innovation for their product design on DIG. And the Material and Chemical Research Lab also step out of their original research boundary and open up another research direction.


The platform greatly reduced the technical barrier for the company’s project manager to come up with a brainstorming idea that’s related to an advanced technology that the manager might have been unfamiliar with. On the other hand, DIG can also help researcher's results be eaily understood by outsiders. And further have more innovative developments.

CITY LUNG

[ Help the city breathe ]

Role:

Project Manager

Duration:

2 months

Director:

Industrial Technology Research Institute of Taiwan - Fang-I Chen

Team member:

Industrial Technology Research Institute of Taiwan - Mei-Ching Shih, Chin-Chih Tai, Yun-Hsin Wang, William T. Y. Huang, Yen-Wu Hsieh, Shuying Cheng, Shih,Chia-Yin Chang, Yu-Hao Huang, A.M.IDEAS - Helen Chen, Wan Shan Lin


Design Concept

Technological developments affect CO2 emission, recovery and reuse in food, beverages and industries, which are superseding each other very rapidly nowadays. However, requirements regarding efficiency of the CO2 capture are becoming more stringent, as are the global warming issue. The proposed adsorption based high surface area fiber recovery unit is novel. Since gas mixture discharged to the atmosphere may contain a high proportion of CO2, a prototype will in all likelihood be tested in a field trial prior to a full scale prototype being designed in the bus. We aim to absorb CO2 as well as providing convinent life for our citizens. Our goal is to balance environment protections and econonmy developments.


How it works to reducing the CO2 in the city air

The Adsorbent Hollow Fiber System is installed on the roof of buses. When the breathing bus drives through the city, CO2 is adsorbed into the adsorbent hollow fiber and therefore provides the city with cleaner air. It can also remove CO2 from inside of the bus to provide passengers a more pleasant ride.


|  Concept Prototype


After the adsorption process or off-duty bus returns to its central station for recovery the high concentration of CO2 (>95%) from the adsorbent hollow fiber system by thermal-vacuum swing process. The recovered CO2 can be reused in the field of food, beverage and industries applications. This novel fiber technology will reduce the emission of Carbon Dioxide (CO2) into the atmosphere and solve one of the key issue of globe warming.


|  Process Flow


Challenge

Although this idea we promoted aiming to protect our environment and create better quality of life, it did not raise any bus producers’ interest. Due to the high cost of updating a new ceiling with hollow fiber on the bus and lack of motivation, bus producers tended to maintain the status quo. So this project is still only in prototype stage, we are still trying to approach more bus companies and governmental institutions to apply this design.



One Mean for Everyone to Help Reverse Global Warming

Although each small car can only contribute a tiny portion, and there will be the trouble of surrendering loaded Lung modules for CO2 extraction, but combined, the efforts are worthwhile and the impact is big.


Global warming is a big challenge for human beings whether some of us brace it or others repel it due to the inevitable inconveniences no matter how insignificant, it’s a matter of time that we will have to adapt these active, mass-scale CO2 sequestration. Each of us as a member of the mass public can participate in the gross effort of CO2 sequestration while enjoying better air quality. It will be perfectly acceptable whether some of us consider we are enjoying good air and collaterally contributing in CO2 sequestration or some others consider it’s the other way round.




About the hollow fiber based systems

The proposal targets the potential development of a super sponge-like adsorbent fibers with the greenhouse gases removal and recycling systems. Adsorbent hollow fiber is like a black hole that can soak up greenhouse elements in the air.


The elements it absorbs in could be controlled manually; the system consisting of filters and fibers os based on the use of uniquely adsorbent low pressure drop device. It is based on polymeric/inorganic materials hybrid technique and to develop a highly efficient, energy saving adsorption unit. It is expected that efficiency and energy saving by the new adsorption system will be up to half that of current technology.


The system consumes less than 20% of the energy demand of a conventional thermal oxidation processes, which can be applied to the removal and recovery of many types of gases (CO2 and Volatile hydrocarbons (VOCs), etc.) and is applicable to a wide range of demanding processes/industries. This system promises significant reductions in environmental emissions, and cost effective recovery and reuse of the valuable solvents and gases.


|  Hollow Fiber



Advantage


・High efficiency

Hollow fibers have high surface area/volume ratio (> 4000 m2/m3) that offer significant improvements in the efficiency of CO2 adsorption/recovery (e.g. the area/volume ratio of state of the art honeycomb multi-channel monoliths is 800 –1000 m2/m3). Another advantage of the fiber adsorption units is that it can be operated on an adsorption/regeneration cycle of 0.5 - 1 h, which allows for a considerable reduction in the adsorbent bed size and it is easily regenerate with low vacuum or thermal swing.


・Low cost manufacturing methods

Many meters of fiber can be spun at any one time, and thus provision of fiber based modules will be price-competitive with existing technologies.


・Lightweight and compact

Enabling flexible configurations of systems to be developed suitable for space-constrained or mobile applications. Our experiments to date have shown that the adsorption performance of the multi-layer hollow fiber module is better than that of an equivalent packed bed of zeolite/carbon adsorbents with around 1/100th of the pressure drop - this allows the adsorption units to be installed in ductwork/limited space without additional gas compression.


・Robust

Use of multi layers means that fibers are handleable and not liable to fracture. As fibers are typically bundled together, any defects occurring during operation should have little or no consequence to the overall efficiency of the system.


Developmental Disabilitied Family (Manfair) Startup Solution


Service Design DEMO

[ Create Tomorrow's hope ]



Role:

Project Manager

Duration:

6 months

Director:

Industrial Technology Research Institute of Taiwan - Fang-I Chen

Cooperate Organization:

Ai-Heng Mental Disability

Co-worker:

Designers from 5% Design Action

Team member:

Industrial Technology Research Institute of Taiwan- Mei-Ching Shih, William T. Y. Huang, Chia-Yin Chang, Yu-Hao Huang, Kevin Yang, Pei-Lin Chen, Chih-Siang Wu


Challenge

In Taiwan, 30% of people with developmental delay come from low income families. Several researches has discovered that due to the immense amount of time and effort to take care of their children, most of parents of the developmental delayed people find it difficult to maintain a full time job, thus these families are often having heavier financial burden.

  • Increase Social Participation :  Disabilitied people usually do not have many chances to interact with people in public places. It is especially crucial for developmentally delayed people to interact with more people, because they need to keep learning how to talk to avoid their brain functions deteriorate. But now developmentally delayed people in Ai-Heng trainning center usually stay in the trainning center and learn to make coffee and sell products. We aim to increase their social participation in this project.
  • Limited job opportunity : People who are disabled are often facing lesser job opportunity. Although the level of disability for each individuals may vary, most of them are found with limited job opportunity.
  • Caring consumptions are hard to maintain : The consumer behavior of buying products to support disabled individuals are often based on the idea of charity, in which the profit stability is hard to maintain, thus limiting the financial support toward the families with developmentally delayed children.

Project goal

The purpose of this project aims to help families with developmental delayed adults to establish family business based on mobile coffee stands, and to raise more social awareness for developmental delayed adults and increasing their social participations.


Observation and In-depth Interview

During the project, I led teammembers in ITRI observed the behaviors of the developmentally delayed people and also kept recording the working environment of the factory for later research. In addition, we interviewed the counselor in the trainning center about their experiences and the challenges they’ve encountered when interact with disability people and their families.


We observed that these disabilitied people can actually make coffee with congruence quality. Our team decided to define "coffee making with family" as our core to develop. And we also went to observed the real condition of the disabilitied people made coffee and record every detail down. In addition, we also researched some exiting mobile coffee stands in Taiwan to understand the operation methods and explore the possibility to use this idea for further design process.


|  The operating detail of existing coffee stands



Brainstorming Workshop

To focus the core problem and further develop related ideas, I hold brainstorming workshops with designers from industries. The workshop was based on the early stage research information and targeted in areas including graphic design, product design and brand marketing. The goal of the workshop was to create solutions for the challenges related to developmentally delayed adults.


Result Guideline

| Customer Journey

The customer journey demonstrates actions of involving stakeholders, including developmentally delayed adults, their parents, and customers. We also listed out some bulletpoints to make sure every designer take care of these details during design process.


Developmentally delayed adults
  • Tools must have stabilized arrangement in terms of types and position.
  • Simple and unified product categories.
  • Retain interactive space for disabilities and their accompanying personnel.
  • Front-end and back-end tasks assignment.


Families of developmentally delayed adults
  • Easily carried equipment.
  • Carry the equipment with electric tricycles.
  • Ensure the customers understand the value and necessity of the waiting time.
Consumers
  • Eye-catching mobile coffee stands.
  • Establish positive impression from interaction with customers.
  • Reduce the waiting time boredom.
  • Establish channels for information dissemination such as electronic media.


Coffee Bike Draft  |

I led product design team to design a electronical coffee tricycle. The mobile coffee stand used electronical power to help people be able to move by carrying over 45 pounds coffee-making tools. And family member of developmentally delayed person to different places and work.




Branding Draft  |

I led graphic design team to design icon and basic identity system. We created several iteractions during the process. And we keep collecting various images and discuss with other group members to make a perfect icon.


Coffee Bike Prototype




Final Prototype  |

We found a tricycle producer who was willing to help us for building this prototype. We also use the new technology in ITRI - Super-thin Electric Assisted Motor - to enhance the disabilities people can carry the coffee-making tools easily.



Final Icon  |

We combined coffee and the smile that we always saw on the disabilities people's face to be the icon. We hope that everyone can enjoy the coffee and smile after receiving the service of coffee stand.

Smile Card  |

In this project, we designed Smile Card for customers to write some sentences for developmentally delayed clerks. This interaction will not disturb the person who is making coffee, and also increase customers' participation during the waiting time.





After the project

The service established from this project has now been passed on and held by the Ai-Heng Training Center for Mental Disability located in Hsin-Chu, Taiwan. The mobile coffee stand project serves a great venturing model of creating job opportunities for the developmentally delayed families and provided firm prospect for related future works on helping the disabled individuals in different corners of the societies.In the future, we hope to optomize the interaction design between customers and developmentally delayed clerks. We will do more usibility testing after the finished products operate on the street. By producing more friendly interactions, we believe the small business will be sustainable and change the life of the developmentally delayed family.
